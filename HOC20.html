HOUR OF CODE 

Vasireddy Venkatadri Institute of Technology takes the lead in any technical activities that help students improve their abilities. As a part of this encouragement, the VVIT GUNTUR ACM student chapter has taught a computer curriculum to local schools and is happy to support ACM's "Hour of Code."

Every computer science education week, the "Hour of Code" is a global campaign that reaches tens of millions of students in 180+ countries with a one-hour introduction to computer science and programming. ACM student chapters all around the world are organizing the week-long event, which will take place from December 6 to 12, 2021.

The VVIT Guntur ACM student chapter, as a local student chapter, requested the ACM India Council for permission to hold an "Hour of Code" event. Following clearance, the VVIT Guntur ACM student branch held an "Hour of Code" event at the following four schools using the website "www.hourofcode.com."

● KENDRIYA VIDYALAYA, GUNTUR

● VIVA THE SCHOOL, NAMBUR

● S.K.B.M.M.C. SCHOOL, GUNTUR

● ZP GIRLS HIGH SCHOOL, NAMBUR

The central objective of the VVIT Guntur ACM student chapter's "Hour of Code" event is to introduce children to the joy of coding. The VVIT Guntur ACM student officers contacted the principals of the above-mentioned schools to acquire permission to hold an "Hour of Code" event in their respective schools. They praised and encouraged the efforts of the VVIT Guntur ACM student chapter

The event was organised by 16 members of the VVIT Guntur ACM student chapter. All members of the student chapter were divided into 4 teams, each with a minimum of four members. The following is the team's formation:

Day wise events:

Date: 6th December 2021

Time: 9.00 AM

School: Kendriya Vidyalaya

The members of the VVIT Guntur ACM Student Chapter continue to move further by conducting the "Hour of Code" event at the Kendriya Vidyalaya school in Guntur. By 8.30 a.m., the chapter members and officers had arrived at the school for the morning session and had made the necessary preparations to begin the "Hour of Code" event at 9:00 a.m. Kendriya Vidyalaya students arrived for the event, and members of the student chapter took control of explaining the tools to the Grade 8 students.
